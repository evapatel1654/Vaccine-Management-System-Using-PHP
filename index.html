    Frequently Asked Questions

    Find answers to some of the most common questions about vaccinations.

    The COVID-19 vaccine is designed to provide immunity against the SARS-CoV-2 virus, which causes COVID-19. It helps your body develop protection against the virus without you having to get the illness.

    Most people aged 6 months and older are eligible for the flu shot. It is especially recommended for individuals who are at higher risk of complications from the flu, such as young children, elderly adults, pregnant women, and people with chronic health conditions.

    Yes, vaccines are thoroughly tested for safety and efficacy before they are approved for public use. They undergo rigorous clinical trials and continuous monitoring to ensure they are safe for the general population.

    Common side effects of vaccines are usually mild and temporary. They can include pain at the injection site, fever, fatigue, and muscle aches. These side effects typically resolve on their own within a few days.
